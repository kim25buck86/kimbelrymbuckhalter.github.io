## About Me

My name is Kimberly M. Buckhalter, and I am an instructional technology facilitator with Jackson Public Schools in Jackson, MS. I have over 5 years of experience in education and 2 years of experience working with teachers and administrators through technology-integration professional development and digital learning initiatives. I have earned an Associate of Arts degree in elementary education from Holmes Community College; a Bachelor of Science degree in child care and family education from Jackson State University; a Master of Arts in Teaching from Jackson State University; and a Specialist in Education degree in school counseling from Jackson State University. I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in Computer Science at the University of Southern Mississippi. My professional interests include educational technology, artificial intelligence in education, computer education, and increasing access to technology opportunities for underrepresented students. My dream research topic is expanding the future computing workforce through graduate education pathways in the South. Through the Dream an Appreciate Program, I am exploring ways to expand community workforce pathways and graduate education opportunities throughout the South.

## About My Project

My research project examines computer science education pathways in Mississippi and their role in developing the future computing workforce. As the demand for computing professionals continues to grow, it is important to understand how educational pathways can prepare students for careers in computer science and related technology fields. This project explores existing research and available data related to computer science education in Mississippi. The study examines participation patterns, educational opportunities, and workforce development trends, with particular attention to factors such as race, ethnicity, gender, geographic location, and access to educational resources.The goal of this research is to identify opportunities for strengthening computer science education pathways in Mississippi and to provide insights that may help expand and diversify the state's future computing workforce.
